A WOMAN WITH HER SKIRT OFF
Han Yong-kyung discussed all the wedding procedures with Dr. Scranton and Pastor Appenzeller, and decided to have a Christian wedding. Finally, the first marriage of Korean Protestants was held at Jeongdong Church in Seoul on Wednesday evening, March 14, 1888, at the officiating of Pastor Appenzeller.

The ceremony was attended by all Methodist missionaries and one from the Presbyterian missionary department. Pastor Appenzeller said, "It is absolutely brave of them to come out in front of people and be a Christian couple." After the wedding, a reception was held, and cake (rice cake) and ice cream were served, and the first wedding ceremony of Korean Christianity was held on March 14, 1888, and this wedding is important in Korean Christian history and modern history in several ways.
