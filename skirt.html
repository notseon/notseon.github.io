A WOMAN WITH HER SKIRT OFF
At the time, American society did not allow women to wear pants, and Amelia Bloomer got her name when she introduced pants called "Bloomer," a 19th century women's rights activist and journalist known as clothes for women's convenience.

Bloomer advocated for women's common sense clothing and convenience, especially against traditional women's clothing, such as a balloon skirt. In 1849, he campaigned for wearing knee-length baggy pants through Lily, a monthly women's newspaper. In 1851, she introduced Bloomer as a new outfit for women in her journal, Lily. Bloomer was a long, baggy pants with comfortable, easy-to-move feature, and had a skirt underneath her pants.

With these new outfits, Amelia Bloomer tried to make women wear more active, realistic, and practical clothes. Her efforts were largely opposed at the time, but they revolutionized women's clothing and influenced later women's clothing designs.

Amelia Bloomer is remembered for her commitment to women's rights and innovation in women's clothing, and her name goes down in history with the term "bloomer," which refers to women's pants.
